ILLINOIS KOLACHE // FIVE CENTURIES OF FILLING

Wanted to look into some historic Illinois foods divided into the different centuries of the state's existence. Also wanted buttery kolache dough that reminds me of my favorite place in Houston that doesn't exist anymore. Inspired by a lot of weird Houston Kolache spots.

THE DOUGH
---------
Brief section on the laminated kolache dough technique:
- Overnight cold ferment
- European-style high-fat butter for lamination (Kerrygold, Plugrá)
- Three letter folds, resting between turns
- Why this dough works across all these fillings — the layers create a moisture barrier, the fat tempers heat, the structure holds wet-ish fillings without collapsing

ERA TABLE // ILLINOIS HISTORICAL FILLINGS ------------------------------------------

ERA              | PEOPLE/CULTURE         | KEY INGREDIENT             | FILLING CONCEPT                       | SOURCE
-----------------|------------------------|----------------------------|---------------------------------------|-------
Pre-1600         | Illiniwek / Potawatomi | Bison, Pawpaw              | Pemmican + dried pawpaw               | [Marquette Journals, 1674]
1600s–1750s      | French Voyageurs       | Whitefish, Bear fat        | Smoked whitefish + hominy             | [Charlevoix letters, 1721]
1750s–1830s      | Frontier settlers      | Salt pork, Honey           | Salt pork + prairie honey             | [Lincoln family accounts]
1930s            | Stockyard immigrants   | Potato, Fatback            | Potato, onion, fried fatback          | [WPA Illinois guides, 1939]
Present          | Chicago metro          | Italian beef, Giardiniera  | Hot giardiniera beef                  | —

[ BISON PEMMICAN + PAWPAW ]
- What is pemmican — rendered bison fat packed with dried meat
- Illiniwek bison hunting and prairie ecology
- Pawpaw: Illinois's lost fruit — custard-banana flavor, short September window

[ SMOKED WHITEFISH + HOMINY ]
- French colonial Illinois country, voyageur diet and portage food
- Lake Michigan whitefish as major trade commodity
- Hominy as the indigenous staple the French adopted

[ SALT PORK + PRAIRIE HONEY ]
- Lincoln-era subsistence farming in central Illinois
- Log cabin foodways — salt pork as the preserved meat staple
- Prairie wildflower honey varieties, bee culture on the frontier

[ POTATO, ONION + FATBACK ]
- Depression-era Chicago, Back of the Yards neighborhood
- Eastern European stockyard worker food culture
- The pierogi connection — essentially the same filling, different wrapper

[ ITALIAN BEEF + HOT GIARDINIERA ]
- Post-war Chicago Italian American community
- The au jus tradition and olive oil-packed giardiniera distinction (olive oil packed, not vinegar — this matters)
- Chuck roast braised in beef broth, shredded fine, mixed with chopped hot giardiniera

[ DANDELION GREENS SAUTÉED IN LARD ]

CHICAGO / ILLINOIS INSPIRED SPICY FILLINGS --------------------------------------------

[ SPORT PEPPER + HOT ITALIAN SAUSAGE ]
- Sport peppers: brined, sharp, the unsung hero of Chicago food
- Crumbled hot Italian sausage, fine-chopped sport peppers, fennel seed
- Reduced completely dry before filling

[ GIARDINIERA + NDUJA ]
- Nduja: spreadable Calabrian spicy pork sausage, essentially already a filling
- Pipe nduja directly with hot olive oil-packed giardiniera

[ STOCKYARD ERA CHILE CON CARNE ]
- Forgotten chili parlor culture of Depression-era Chicago
- Dry-packed beef with dried ancho and guajillo chili, no beans
- Concentrated almost to a paste — mole-adjacent in texture

INTERNATIONAL FILLINGS // FAMILIAR TO OBSCURE
----------------------------------------------

[ DUCK RILLETTE ]
- Pulled pork, Swiss, pickle, mustard
- Brine from pickle prevents richness overload
- Mustard blooms inside the dough during baking

[ CUBANO ]
- Pulled pork, Swiss, pickle, mustard
- Brine from pickle prevents richness overload
- Mustard blooms inside the dough during baking

[ BÁNH MÌ INSPIRED ]
- Pork pâté or lemongrass pork
- Quick-pickled daikon and carrot, jalapeño

[ LAMB AND FETA ]
- Greek-adjacent, very approachable
- Ground lamb, oregano, lemon zest, crumbled feta

[ MOLE NEGRO + PULLED TURKEY ]
- Dried chiles, chocolate, turkey shredded fine
- Labor intensive but filling is fully make-ahead
- Deep complexity that stands up to the butter

[ BERBERE SPICED LENTIL + LAMB ]
- Ethiopian direction, very aromatic
- Berbere spice blend has enough depth for laminated dough
- Small amount of ayib (fresh Ethiopian cheese) for creaminess

[ BÖREK INSPIRED ]
- Turkish, ground lamb or beef
- Allspice, pine nuts, currants
- Sweet-savory currant + pine nut combination

[ MORCILLA + CARAMELIZED ONION ]
- Spanish or South American blood sausage direction
- Dry crumbled morcilla, caramelized onion, smoked paprika

[ FERMENTED BLACK BEAN + PORK BELLY ]
- Douchi (fermented black beans) with finely minced pork belly
- Ginger, Shaoxing wine reduced completely dry

[ NDUJA + CALABRIAN HONEY ]
- Spreadable Calabrian spicy pork with Calabrian chili honey
- Honey caramelizes slightly against the pastry interior

[ MAPO TOFU INSPIRED ]
- Extra-firm tofu pressed completely dry, crumbled
- Ground pork, doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste)
- Sichuan peppercorn
- Need to dry before filling

[ HARISSA LAMB ]
- North African, straightforward
- Ground lamb, rose harissa, preserved lemon rind finely minced
- Ras el hanout

[ SCOTCH BONNET + SALTFISH ]
- Jamaican direction
- Salt cod rehydrated and flaked, scotch bonnet, thyme, scallion
- Cooked completely dry before filling

[ SICHUAN MALA BUTTER DOUGH ]
- Infuse lamination butter with Sichuan peppercorn and dried er jing tiao chili before laminating
- Simple pork and ginger filling inside

[ PIRI PIRI CHICKEN ]
- Mozambican-Portuguese
- Chicken thigh cooked in piri piri sauce until almost jammy
- African bird's eye chili, lemon, garlic

[ KIVIAK ADJACENT ]
- Greenlandic Inuit fermented seabird
- Long-aged duck prosciutto or duck ham, fermented black garlic, rendered duck fat
- Arctic funky aged-poultry

[ SURSTRÖMMING INSPIRED ]
- Cold-smoked herring, crème fraîche, dill
- small amount of fish sauce reduced into the mixture

DRINKS PAIRING
--------------
- Green River
- Malted milk — Chicago soda fountain culture 1920s-40s, dark chocolate malt with pinch of malt powder pairs well with bison or salt pork fillings
- Egg cream — appropriate with whitefish filling (milk, chocolate syrup, seltzer — no egg, no cream)
- Switchel — colonial/frontier era, apple cider vinegar, water, ginger, molasses or honey, cuts through fat of any filling, right era for salt pork kolache

WITH RED WINE
- Côtes du Rhône or Grenache-based for duck rillette
- Nero d'Avola for morcilla or nduja
- Malbec for bison pemmican
